Explore Principality of Andorra

Andorra is a sovereign landlocked microstate on the Iberian Peninsula, in the eastern Pyrenees, bordered by France to the north and Spain to the south. With an area of 468 square kilometers, it is the sixth smallest country in Europe.

Known for its stunning mountain scenery, Andorra is a popular destination for skiing and hiking. The capital, Andorra la Vella, is the highest capital city in Europe at an elevation of 1,023 meters.

Andorra has a rich history dating back to Charlemagne and has maintained its independence for over a millennium. The official language is Catalan, though Spanish, French, and Portuguese are also commonly spoken.

Visa Guidelines for Andorra

  • All visitors to Andorra must have a valid passport or travel document.
  • Since Andorra is not part of the Schengen Area, separate visa requirements apply.
  • Holders of Schengen visas can enter Andorra but must ensure their visa allows re-entry to France or Spain.
  • Work visas require a pre-arranged employment contract with an Andorran company.
  • Processing time for visas is typically 15-30 working days.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to process a visa application?
Processing times vary by visa type. Tourist visas typically take 10-15 working days, while work and student visas may take 20-30 working days. You can check your application status using our online system.
Can I extend my visa while in Andorra?
Visa extensions are possible in certain circumstances, such as medical emergencies or unexpected events. You must apply for an extension before your current visa expires at the Immigration Office in Andorra la Vella.
Do I need a visa if I have a Schengen visa?
Andorra is not part of the Schengen Area. While holders of Schengen visas can enter Andorra, they must ensure their visa allows multiple entries to return to France or Spain. Some travelers may still need a separate Andorran visa depending on their nationality.
What documents do I need for a work visa?
For a work visa, you need: a valid passport, employment contract from an Andorran company, medical certificate, criminal background check, completed application form, passport-sized photographs, and proof of accommodation in Andorra.
Can I work in Andorra with a tourist visa?
No, it is illegal to work in Andorra with a tourist visa. You must obtain a proper work visa before engaging in any employment activities. Violators may face deportation and bans from re-entering Andorra.
